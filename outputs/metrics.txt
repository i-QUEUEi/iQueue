WHY THESE MODELS
LinearRegression is the simplest baseline and shows whether the pattern is close to linear.
RandomForest handles nonlinear interactions, noise, and mixed feature scales without manual scaling.
GradientBoosting is included because it can capture residual structure with fewer trees than bagging models.

WHY NOT OTHER OPTIONS
We did not rely on only a mean predictor because it ignores hour/day structure.
We did not add heavy preprocessing like scaling or one-hot encoding because the inputs are already numeric and tree models are scale-insensitive.
We avoided overly complex models such as neural networks because the dataset is small and the signal is mostly tabular pattern learning.

PREPROCESSING CHOICES
Parsed dates and derived week_of_month to preserve within-month seasonality.
Filtered negative waiting times and queue lengths, then dropped missing rows for a clean training set.
Kept the engineered lag and peak features because they encode the queue dynamics directly.
